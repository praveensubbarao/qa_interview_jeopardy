Interactive QA KPI reference — definitions, formulas, targets, and a sample executive dashboard

Automation coverage %

Efficiency

Plain english: out of every 100 test cases your team owns, how many run automatically without a human clicking anything? Higher = less manual work every release.

(Automated test cases ÷ Total test cases) × 100

Example

800 automated ÷ 1000 total = 80%

Industry target

70–85% mature org

Cadence

Monthly to leadership

Praveen story: Care.com went from ~30% to 80%+ automation coverage during the Playwright migration — that's the number to cite.

Test execution time

Efficiency

Plain english: how long does the full test suite take to run? A 4-hour suite means developers wait half a workday for feedback. A 20-minute suite means they get feedback before their coffee gets cold.

Time from pipeline trigger → all tests complete (minutes)

Smoke suite target

Under 10 min

Regression target

Under 45 min

Praveen result

60% reduction

Manual testing hours saved per release

Efficiency

Plain english: before automation, how many engineer-hours did it take to manually test a release? Subtract what it takes now. That number, multiplied by salary cost, is the ROI you present to leadership.

(Baseline manual hours − Current manual hours) per release cycle

Example

200 hrs → 40 hrs = 160 hrs saved

ROI formula

Hours saved × avg hourly rate

Cadence

Per release, rolling quarterly

CI/CD pipeline pass rate

Efficiency

Plain english: every time code is pushed, the pipeline runs. What percentage of those runs pass on first attempt without a human restarting them? Low pass rate = flaky tests = developers stop trusting the pipeline and start ignoring failures.

(Pipeline runs passing on first attempt ÷ Total pipeline runs) × 100

Industry target

Above 95%

Praveen result

90% improvement

Red flag

Below 85% = trust problem

Defect escape rate

Quality

Plain english: out of every 10 bugs that exist in your product, how many did your testing process miss and let through to customers? This is the single most important quality KPI. Zero tolerance at American Tower — a bug in financial systems has compliance consequences.

(Bugs found in production ÷ (Bugs found in testing + Bugs found in production)) × 100

Example

5 prod bugs ÷ (45 + 5) = 10%

Industry target

Below 10%

Lower is better

Above 20% = urgent action

American Tower context: for Oracle EBS and Salesforce financial workflows, target below 2%. Any escape in billing or lease data is a compliance risk, not just a bug.

Defect detection rate

Quality

Plain english: the flip side of escape rate. Out of all the bugs that existed, what percentage did your testing find before release? Think of it as your batting average — how often does the test suite catch what it's supposed to catch.

(Bugs found in testing ÷ (Bugs in testing + Bugs in production)) × 100

Relationship to escape rate

Detection rate = 100% − Escape rate

Industry target

Above 90%

Cadence

Per release, trending monthly

Test flakiness rate

Quality

Plain english: a flaky test is one that sometimes passes and sometimes fails on the exact same code — like a fire alarm that goes off randomly. Flaky tests destroy trust. When engineers see enough false alarms they start ignoring the pipeline — and real bugs slip through.

(Tests with inconsistent results on same codebase ÷ Total automated tests) × 100

Target

Below 2%

Action trigger

Above 5% = quarantine + fix sprint

Cadence

Weekly automated report

Cost of quality (CoQ)

Quality

Plain english: fixing a bug in production costs 30x more than fixing it in development. CoQ compares what you spend on prevention (testing, automation, reviews) versus what you spend on failure (production incidents, rework, support costs). The goal is to shift spend left.

Prevention cost + Appraisal cost + Internal failure cost + External failure cost

Good ratio

70% prevention, 30% failure

Bad ratio

Mostly failure cost = reactive org

Use it for

Annual budget justification

Critical path coverage %

Risk

Plain english: not all workflows are equal. A bug in your company logo is annoying. A bug in lease billing at American Tower is a financial and compliance emergency. Critical path coverage asks — are your most important, highest-risk workflows automated? 100% total coverage means nothing if your critical paths have gaps.

(Critical workflows with automated coverage ÷ Total critical workflows defined) × 100

Target

100% of critical paths

AMT critical paths

Lease billing, tenant workflow, Oracle EBS

First 30-day action

Define and document what "critical" means

Release gate pass rate

Risk

Plain english: before any code goes to production, it has to pass a quality gate — a set of automated tests that must all pass. This KPI tracks how often releases pass that gate on the first attempt. If it's low, either your code quality is poor or your gate is poorly designed.

(Releases passing quality gate on first submission ÷ Total release attempts) × 100

Target

Above 90%

Too high (above 99%)

Gate may be too loose — not catching enough

Too low (below 70%)

Dev process or code quality issue

Mean time to detect (MTTD)

Risk

Plain english: when a bug is introduced into the codebase, how long before your testing finds it? A bug introduced Monday that's caught by the pipeline Monday afternoon is cheap. The same bug found by a customer Friday is expensive. MTTD measures how quickly your safety net responds.

Average time from bug introduction → automated test failure (hours)

World class

Under 1 hour (CI on every commit)

Acceptable

Same business day

Problematic

More than 24 hours

Mean time to recover (MTTR)

Risk

Plain english: when something does break in production, how quickly does the team restore normal service? This is less about preventing failure and more about resilience. American Tower runs 24/7 infrastructure — MTTR is a direct measure of operational risk.

Average time from incident detection → service restored to normal (hours)

Industry target

Under 1 hour for P1

QA's role

Hotfix regression suites that run in minutes

Care.com result

Zero downtime on major migrations
